National Talent Search Examination (NTSE)

The NTSE is an exam conducted by NCERT for students in class 10. It aims to identify talented students for scholarships. The exam has two stages, and students who perform well are awarded scholarships to pursue higher education in subjects like Science and Social Studies.

  • Structure: Two stages: Stage I (state-level) and Stage II (national-level).
  • Eligibility: Students in class 10 with a minimum of 60% marks in their 9th grade subjects.
  • Scholarship: Provided based on performance across both stages.

National Level Science Talent Search Examination (NSTSE)

NSTSE is an exam focusing on Mathematics and Science for students from class 2 to 12. It helps students improve their conceptual understanding through a more analytical approach.

  • Eligibility: Open to students from class 2 to 12.
  • Subjects: Mathematics and General Science.
  • Awards: Cash prizes, medals, and certificates for top performers.

Indian National Olympiad (INO)

INO is an Olympiad aimed at recognizing students with exceptional talent in subjects like Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and Junior Science.

  • Eligibility: Students in grades 8 to 10.
  • Stages: Includes various stages, from National Standard Examination (NSE) to participation in the International Olympiad.
  • Purpose: Encourages intellectual competition and provides educational camps for selected students.

Science Olympiad Foundation (SOF)

SOF organizes multiple Olympiads in Science, Mathematics, English, and Computer Science for students to improve their logical reasoning and problem-solving abilities.

  • Competitions: National Science Olympiad (NSO), International English Olympiad (IEO), International Mathematics Olympiad (IMO), National Cyber Olympiad (NCO), and more.
  • Levels: Competitions are conducted at school, zonal, state, and international levels.
  • Prizes: Cash rewards, gifts, and scholarships.

GeoGenius Geography Exam

GeoGenius is a geography competition where students from class 2 to 12 can participate and showcase their knowledge about the world.

  • Eligibility: Open to students from class 2 to 12.
  • International Recognition: Winners can represent India at the International Geography Olympiad.
  • Awards: Prizes and recognition at national and international levels.

Kishore Vaigyanik Protsahan Yojana (KVPY)

KVPY is a prestigious exam encouraging students to pursue research in science, engineering, and medicine.

  • Eligibility: Class 11 (SA stream) and Class 12 (SX stream) students.
  • Selection Process: Includes an aptitude test and a personal interview.
  • Fellowships: Awarded to top candidates to support their higher education in science.
